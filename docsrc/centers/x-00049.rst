Suppose that P and Q are distinct points in the plane of a triangle ABC . Let PA = reflection of P in :term:`line <line>` AQ, let QA = reflection of Q in :term:`line <line>` AP, and let MA = midpoint of segment PAQA. Define MB and MC cyclically. CÃ©sar Lozada found that if Q = :term:`isogonal conjugate <isogonal conjugate>` of P, then the locus of P for which MAMBMC is :term:`perspective <perspective>` to ABC is the union of a :term:`cubic <cubic>` and 6 circles: specifically, the McCay :term:`cubic <cubic>` (K003), the circles {B,C,B',C'}}, {C,A,C',A'}}, {A,B,A',B'}}, and the circles {B,C,A'}}, {C,A,B'}}, {A,B,C'}}, where A',B',C' are the excenters of ABC. Moreover, if P = :ref:`X(3) <X(3)>` and Q = :ref:`X(4) <X(4)>`, then MAMBMC is not only :term:`perspective <perspective>`, but homothetic, to ABC, and the :term:`center <center>` of homothety is :ref:`X(49) <X(49)>`. See Hyacinthos 23265, June 1, 2015.
