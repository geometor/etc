Let A'B'C' be the :term:`cevian triangle <cevian triangle>` of :ref:`X(1) <X(1)>`. Let A&Prime; be the centroid of triangle AB'C', and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Then the lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in :ref:`X(37) <X(37)>`. (Eric Danneels, Hyacinthos 7892, 9/13/03)

A simple construction of :ref:`X(37) <X(37)>` as a :term:`crosspoint <crosspoint>` can be generalized as follows: let DEF be the medial triangle of ABC and let A'B'C' be the :term:`cevian triangle <cevian triangle>` of a :term:`point <point>` U other than the centroid, :ref:`X(2) <X(2)>`. The :term:`crosspoint <crosspoint>` of :ref:`X(2) <X(2)>` and U is then the :term:`point <point>` of concurrence of lines LD,ME,NF, where L,M,N are the respective midpoints of AA', BB', CC'. If U=u : v : w (trilinears), then :term:`crosspoint <crosspoint>`(:ref:`X(2) <X(2)>`,U) = b/w+c/v : c/u+a/w : a/v+b/u, assuming that uvw is nonzero. In particular, if U=:ref:`X(1) <X(1)>`, then the :term:`crosspoint <crosspoint>` is :ref:`X(37) <X(37)>`. (Seiichi Kirikami, July 10, 2011)



:ref:`X(37) <X(37)>` = perspector of ABC and the medial triangle of the incentral triangle of ABC. (Randy Hutson, August 23, 2011)



:ref:`X(37) <X(37)>` = :term:`center <center>` of the Hofstadter ellipse E(1/2); see :ref:`X(359) <X(359)>`. This is the incentral inellipse, which is the trilinear square of the antiorthic axis. (Randy Hutson, August 9, 2014)

Let A' be the :term:`trilinear pole <trilinear pole>` of the tangent to the Apollonius circle where it meets the A-excircle, and define B' and C' cyclically. The triangle A'B'C' is homothetic to ABC at :ref:`X(37) <X(37)>`. (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)

Let Oa be the A-extraversion of the Conway circle (the circle centered at the A-excenter and passing through A, with radius sqrt(ra^2 + s^2), where ra is the A-exradius). Let La be the radical axis of the circumcircle and Oa. Let A' = Lb&cap;Lc, B' = Lc&cap;La, C' = La&cap;Lb. The lines AA', BB', CC' concur in :ref:`X(37) <X(37)>`. (Randy Hutson, April 9, 2016)
