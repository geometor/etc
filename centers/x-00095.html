Let A’B’C’ be the symmedial triangle. Let La be the reflection of line B’C’ in line BC, and define Lb and Lc cyclically. Let A&Prime; = Lb&cap;Lc, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(95). (Randy Hutson, August 19, 2015)

Let A’ be the intersection, other than A, of the circumcircle and the branch of the Lucas cubic that contains A, and define B’ and C’ cyclically. The triangle A’B’C’ is here introduced as the Lucas triangle (not to be confused with the Lucas central triangle).
