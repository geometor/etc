Notes

Let A’B’C’ be the anticomplementary triangle. Let A&Prime; be the inverse-in-anticomplementary-circle of A, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The lines A’A&Prime;, B’B&Prime;, C’C&Prime; concur in X(69). (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)

Let A’B’C’ be the anticomplementary triangle. Let A&Prime; be the orthogonal projection of A’ on line BC, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The lines AA&Prime;, BB&Prime;, CC&Prime; concur in X(69). (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)

Let A’B’C’ be the half-altitude triangle. Let A&Prime; be the trilinear pole of line B’C’, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Let A* be the trilinear pole of line B&Prime;C&Prime;, and define B* and C* cyclically. The lines AA*, BB*, CC* concur in X(69). (Randy Hutson, February 10, 2016)

Let A2B2C2 be the 2nd Conway triangle. Let A’ be the cevapoint of B2 and C2, and define B’ and C’ cyclically. The lines AA’, BB’, CC’ concur in X(69). (Randy Hutson, December 10, 2016)

X(69) is the barycentric multiplier for the MacBeath circumconic. (The barycentric product of X(69) and the circumcircle is the MacBeath circumconic.) (Randy Hutson, August 19, 2019)

Let OA be the circle centered at the A-vertex of the 1st Ehrmann triangle and passing through A; define OB and OC cyclically. X(69) is the radical center of OA, OB, OC. (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2020)
