Notes

Let P be a point on the circumcircle, and let LP be its Steiner line. The locus of the orthopole of LP, as P varies, is an ellipse with center X(4) and perspector X(66). (Randy Hutson, March 29, 2020)

In the plane of a triangle ABC, let A’B’C’ = anticomplementary triangle; Oa = circle with diameter BC, and define Ob and Oc cyclically; Ab = A’BC’&cap;Oa, and define Bc and Ca cyclically; Ac = B’CA’&cap;Oa, and define Ba and Cb cyclically; A&Prime;= BcBa&cap;CaCb, and define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. The triangle A&Prime;B&Prime;C&Prime; is perspective to ABC, and the perspector is X(66). (Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, April 15, 2021)
