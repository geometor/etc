Let A’B’C’ be the orthic triangle. Let B’C’A&Prime; be the triangle similar to ABC such that segment A’A&Prime; crosses the line B’C’. Define B&Prime; and C&Prime; cyclically. Let A’ be the isogonal conjugate of A wrt the A-extouch triangle. Define B’ and C’ cyclically. The lines AA’, BB’, CC’ concur in X(65). (Randy Hutson, August 30, 2020)

In the plane of a triangle ABC, let Ba = reflection of A in the external angular bisector of angle B, and define Cb and Ac cyclically; Ca = reflection of A in the external angular bisector of angle C, and define Ab and Bc cyclically; Pa = AcBc&cap;AbCb, and define Pb and Pc cyclically; Ka = AbBa&cap;AcCa, and define Kb and Kc cyclically. Then PaPbPc and ABC, and also KaKbKc and ABC, are perspective, and the perspector is X(65). (Dasari Naga Vijay Krishna, June 19, 2021)
